README - Customer Segmentation Project

Overview

This project applies K-means clustering to segment customers based on Age, Annual Income, and Spending Score. The purpose is to identify distinct customer groups and derive actionable insights for marketing and business strategy.

Dataset

Features Used: Age, Annual Income (k$), Spending Score (1–100)

Size: Customer demographic and behavioral records

Goal: Group customers into clusters that reflect spending behavior and demographic patterns.


Methodology


Preprocessing: Cleaned and standardized data.

Clustering Algorithm: K-means with different values of k.

Feature Selection: Visualizations created using:

Age vs Spending Score (4 clusters)
Annual Income vs Spending Score (5 clusters)


Results

Age vs Spending Score:

-Cluster 1: Older customer, moderate spending.
-Cluster 2: Younger to middle-aged customers ,high spending scores. (premium segment).
-Cluster 3: Older, low spending (conservative buyers).
-Cluster 4: Younger, low spending (price-sensitive).


Annual Income vs Spending Score:

-Cluster 1: Moderate income and moderate spending scores (budget-conscious).
-Cluster 2: High-income customers with high spending scores (VIP segment).
-Cluster 3: Low-income customers with high spending scores (engaged buyers).
-Cluster 4: Low income customers with low spending scores (Value driven).
-Cluster 5: High income customers with low spending scores. (Conservative buyers).


Insights

-High-value clusters should be prioritized with premium offerings and loyalty programs.
-Low-income clusters require affordable bundles and cost-effective engagement.
-High-income low spenders represent untapped potential; strategies should focus on trust-building and exclusivity.
-Moderate segments provide stability and can be nurtured gradually.


Recommendations

Tailor marketing campaigns to each cluster.
Use personalized communication strategies.
Focus resources on high-value and high-potential clusters.
Maintain cost efficiency with low-value segments.


Conclusion

This project demonstrates how clustering can uncover meaningful customer segments. By aligning business strategies with these insights, companies can maximize ROI, improve customer satisfaction, and strengthen long-term loyalty.
